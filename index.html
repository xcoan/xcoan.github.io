About Me

I am currently a student at the Polytechnic Institute at Purdue Univerity, pursuing an undergraduate degree in Organization Leadership. My deep down passion is computer programming, specifically in web development. I hope to one day pursue a career in informational technology as either a systems analyst, full-stack engineer, or project manager.

I am on a constant journey to learn more about computing and information technology. My skill set lies primarily withing full stack web development. I am a proud user of Ruby on Rails and Express.js, but am always looking to learn new frameworks.

My Background

I grew up in Michigan City, Indiana, where I attended the local high school. During my Junior year, I worked an internship for the school system IT department, where I set up many of the computer labs for the schools and pushed disk images to computers. I also aided in maintaining server-switch boxes the school's network ran on, which included replacing batteries and electrical connectors, and replacing network cables.


In the summer of 2016, I took part in the Xtern Program in Indianapolis, Indiana. Through the Xtern Program, I met 130 other college students who were interested in working in computing fields. Thanks to Xtern, I had an amazing internship with Octiv.

At Octiv, my work was focused around Ruby on Rails and Ember.js. Over I worked extensively with I18N and L10N with YAML files to allow the application to be translated and localized into various languages around the world. Using a chron job, I modified our internal API to automatically purge month-old data from our Redis NoSQL data cache. I also worked on preventing potential XSS and CSRF attacks.

I wrote a blog post about my experience with Xtern and Octiv on the octiv website, which you can view here.

For the upcoming Summer of 2017, I plan on returning to my internship at Octiv, once again through the Xtern Program.

Featured Projects

Infract IO data aggregation and visualization

A web app written for the 2016 Indianapolis Civic Hackathon, and is still under active development. It is built using Node Express on top of MongoDB. Using the Google Maps API and a GEOJson file recieved via an AJAX call to OpenIndy, the app plots data of crimes onto a map of Indianapolis, allowing for efficient and effective data aggregation and visualization for citizens and lawmakers alike. Infract IO recieved second place in the hackathon. Work on Infract IO was done in collaboration with fellow hacker Aaron Krueger.

Medlog record keeping for medical professionals

Developed in November 2016 for ArchHacks. Medlog is a server system built on Rails that allows Hospitals and other medical institutions to easily track basic information on their patients. The goal of the application is to minimize the amount of time medical professionals spend time doing paperwork, allowing more time to see patients and take care of emergencies. Medlog is currently undergoing development to be submitted to the GE Health Cloud Innovation Challenge.
